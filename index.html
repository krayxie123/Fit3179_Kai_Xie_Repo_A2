VCE School Results Across Victoria

VCE results have been a big deal in Australia

Victoria is also known as the education state. Nevertheless, just how well educated is the entire state? Is there disparity between regions and inequality? Moreover, how has this led to higher education admissions? Let's Find out!

Median VCE Study Scores by Suburbs

Understanding VCE Scores:

• 30 is the median score for the entire state for a subject

• 50 is the highest achievable mark within Victoria

• Scores that are above 40 are considered to be excellent

• Median scores could vary significantly based on school and region


Suburb Median Score Relationships:
As we observe and increase the VCE score threshold, we can see just how the median scores vary for each suburb in terms of their location from the metropolitan areas. Setting higher thresholds reveals that metropolitan areas and regions closer to the city tend to maintain higher median scores. In contrast, regional and remote areas show much lower performance compared to regions closer to the city. This pattern demonstrates the correlation between geographic proximity to metropolitan centres and educational outcomes, which highlights the potential resource disparities that impact VCE performance across Victoria.

Regional VCE Performance Analysis

Regional insights also reveal significant educational disparities across Victoria's four major regions. South Eastern Victoria demonstrates the highest VCE performance, while other regions show varying levels of educational outcomes. Regional Performance Patterns: This bubble chart shows the relationship between high-achieving students (who obtain 40+ study scores) and university admission rates across Victorian regions. Each bubble represents a school, with the size indicating a 40+ study score percentage and the position showing university admission rates.

Analysis: We can observe that for southeastern locations, the schools perform much better than other regions, having fewer schools that have lower university admissions and the majority of schools that are achieving well in terms of the VCE also does correlate with a higher university admission rate. The lowest performing will be the south western region, where it displays much more schools that have lower university admission rates as well as having fewer schools that have higher university admissions, along with the lower percentage of 40+ scores in those schools.

Regional Tertiary Application Trends Over Time

Regional Performance Patterns: This trellis time series chart reveals a concerning trend, where tertiary application rates are actually decreasing across all Victorian regions over time, which contradicts Victoria's reputation as the "Education State." Each small chart represents a region's declining trend, highlighting how regional differences in educational resources impact post-secondary education planning and opportunities.

Regional Disparities: As we can observe from the trellis diagram, the regions all exhibit a downwards trend in tertiary applications, but the region most impacted is the south-western and north-western Victoria, which could represent a lack of quality tertiary education institutions and a higher emphasis on trades. These locations also demonstrated the lowest achievements compared to the other regions present. Also, they observed the highest rates of lower study scores.

Conclusion: Educational Disparities Across Victoria

Key Findings: This comprehensive analysis reveals significant educational disparities across Victoria, challenging the state's reputation as the "Education State." The data demonstrates clear patterns of inequality that correlate with geographic location, school sector, and socioeconomic factors.

Geographic region differences: It is shown clearly that there is a clear correlation between higher vce results and tertiary applications. Metropolitan areas consistently outperform regional and remote areas, with metro schools showing higher VCE scores and tertiary application rates. In contrast, non-metro schools show more variability and lower median performance.

Education Sector imbalances: Independent schools demonstrate the highest performance levels, followed by Catholic schools, with Government schools showing the widest range of outcomes. This reflects resource disparities and different educational approaches across sectors.

Regional Variations: Southeastern Victoria shows the highest performance, while other regions demonstrate varying levels of educational outcomes. The trellis time series diagram also reveals concerning trends of declining tertiary application rates across all regions over time.
