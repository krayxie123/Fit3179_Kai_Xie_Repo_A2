About VCE Results in Victoria

VCE results have been a great big deal in Australia - Victoria is known as the education state. Just how well educated is the entire state? Is there disparity between regions and inequality? Let's investigate.

Understanding VCE Scores:
  • 30 is the median score for all states for a subject
  • 50 is the maximum mark achievable
  • Scores above 40 are considered excellent
  • Median scores vary significantly by school and region

Regional VCE Performance Analysis

Distribution patterns and average scores across Victorian regions reveal significant educational disparities.

Regional Performance Insights

South Eastern Victoria is well known to have some of the best schools in Victoria and is the most educated region, consistently excelling in VCE performance. The density plot shows the distribution patterns of scores across regions, while the bar chart reveals the average performance levels.

The distributions show that larger metropolitan regions tend to have more diverse score ranges, with both high-performing and struggling schools, while smaller regional areas often show more concentrated performance patterns. This reflects the complex relationship between geographic location, socioeconomic factors, and educational outcomes across Victoria.

Access to Education: Location and Regionality

Metropolitan areas have much more resources compared to regional Victoria. This disparity is clearly reflected in the educational outcomes and school sector distribution. Metro areas typically have greater access to:

  • Diverse school options (Government, Catholic, Independent)
  • Specialized programs and resources
  • Highly qualified teachers and staff
  • Advanced facilities and technology
  • Extracurricular opportunities

Regional areas often face challenges with limited school choices, fewer resources, and geographic isolation. The density plots reveal how these resource disparities translate into different performance patterns and educational opportunities across Victoria's metropolitan and non-metropolitan regions.

Education Sector Distribution & Performance

Government schools (blue) are the most numerous and widely distributed across Victoria, while Catholic schools (gold) and Independent schools (green) tend to be more concentrated in metropolitan areas. The bubble chart shows the relationship between VCE performance and tertiary application rates, with bubble size representing the number of VCE students.

The pie chart illustrates the overall distribution of school sectors, while the scatter plot reveals how distance from Melbourne CBD affects performance. Independent and Catholic schools often show higher VCE scores and tertiary application rates, reflecting different educational approaches and student demographics.
